I would first like to read to you some passages from the Bible found at Ephesians chapter 3, verses 14 through 19.
This will also establish the theme for my comments this month.
Ephesians 3, starting with verse 14, Paul said: “For this reason I bend my knees to the Father, “to whom every family in heaven and on earth owes its name.
“I pray that he may grant you through the abundance of his glory “to be made mighty in the man you are inside, “with power through his spirit, “and that through your faith you may have the Christ dwell in your hearts with love.
“May you be rooted and established on the foundation, “in order that with all the holy ones you may be thoroughly able “to comprehend fully what is the breadth “and length and height and depth, “and to know the love of the Christ, which surpasses knowledge, “so that you may be filled with all the fullness that God gives.” Now, verse 15 is the inspiration for my theme, namely, “Glorify Jehovah—The One to Whom Every Family Owes Its Name.” The family unit is under a vicious attack in these last days.

Do you remember those verses we read in Ephesians chapter 3?
Verse 16 said that the One to whom every family owes its name, Jehovah, would ‘grant us to be made mighty in the person we are inside.’ Yes, Jehovah can give us the internal spiritual strength to fight back against Satan’s efforts to destroy the family.
How?
By being immersed in God’s Word.
In particular, by imitating the loving way that Jesus treated his disciples.
Did you note that Ephesians 3:17 said we are to “have the Christ dwell in [our] hearts with love”?
Yes, if each of us cherishes the Christ in our hearts to the point that he dwells, or resides, there constantly, our family will not only survive but it will truly thrive.
This will be so evident that verse 17 further states that we will “be rooted and established on the foundation.” We will be solid!
And keep in mind that 1 Corinthians 3:11 says that the “foundation” on which we build “is Jesus Christ.” Then verse 18 of Ephesians chapter 3 indicates that our spiritual peripheral vision will be enhanced.
We will not have spiritual blind spots that could allow the spirit of the world to creep into the family.
That verse says that we will “be thoroughly able “to comprehend fully what is the breadth and length and height and depth.” All aspects of Jesus’ personality will come into play as we interact with family members.
Finally, verse 19 sums up the result when it says that we would then have come “to know the love of the Christ, which surpasses knowledge.” Granted, knowledge is important, but it is the application of Christlike love that will preserve our family, keeping it vibrantly intact.
Family life in these critical last days could be likened to those who suffer from diabetes.
It is believed that diabetes is caused by both genetic and environmental factors.
Similarly, all in the family have inherited sin and imperfection from our first human parents, Adam and Eve, as if genetically.
However, we are also exposed to the ever more toxic environmental factors of Satan’s evil world.
Most who suffer from a serious case of diabetes need to use two methods to successfully relieve the symptoms and lead a normal life.
They have to watch their diet and, in many cases, take an injection of insulin daily.
Spiritually, we need to avoid the sickly, sweet junk food that the world promotes in movies, talk shows, the Internet, and in print.
Just as with the person who has diabetes, this requires a permanent lifestyle change from what we might have exposed ourselves to in the past.
In addition to avoiding unwholesome junk food from Satan’s system, we also need to take daily “injections” of God’s Word and other wholesome provisions.
This has become more and more important the deeper we get into these last days.
How has our organization provided this when it comes to family life?

That has proved to be a blessing for both young ones and their parents.
