We have selected the theme “Be a Champion of Truth!” You may have heard of the word “champion” used in the context of sports.
What is a champion?
The word can have different meanings.
The word is related to the French word champ, meaning “field.” That word has its origin in the Latin word campus, also meaning “field” or “battlefield.” The Bible speaks of a champion on a battlefield in 1 Samuel 17:4: “Then a champion came out from the camps of the Philistines; his name was Goliath.” Goliath was a champion for a wrong cause, namely, to take the lead in defeating the ancient Israelites in battle.
In our modern time, according to one definition, a champion is “an ardent defender “or supporter of a cause or another person.” Christians today are ardent defenders and supporters of the truth.
The Watchtower of January 15, 2012, said on page 8: “Other champions of Bible truth rose up in the centuries that followed.” Years before, The Watchtower said: “This magazine will never shrink in fear from championing the truth!” “The Watchtower has been championing ‘the cause of Kingdom truth.’” So, are you a champion?
In a sports competition, only one individual gets to be the champion.
But in the case of true Christians, all can be champions of the truth.
All Christians are to defend the truth and become conquerors —winners.
It’s necessary to defend the truth because in today’s world truth is being attacked and distorted.
We are surrounded by a sea of lies and misrepresentations.
