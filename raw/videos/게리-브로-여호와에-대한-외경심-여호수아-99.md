# 게리 브로: 여호와에 대한 외경심 (여호수아 9:9)

## 자막 전사본 (transcript) — ⚠️ E 자막 (한국어 자막 미발행)

The whole obligation and privilege of mankind is to develop a relationship with our God and Maker, Jehovah.
But the question is, How do we develop that relationship?
And then, How do we keep it?
Well, let’s start with, How do we develop it?
If you open to Ecclesiastes 12:13, note the two components mentioned in this verse —Ecclesiastes 12:13.
It says: “The conclusion of the matter, “everything having been heard, is: “Fear the true God and keep his commandments, for this is the whole obligation of man.” So the obligation begins with fearing the true God and then keeping his commandments.
So, what does it mean to fear?
What kind of fear are we talking about?
The Insight book says this: “The fear of God is healthful; “it is an awe “and a profound reverence for the Creator “and a wholesome dread of displeasing him.
“This fear of incurring his displeasure “is a result of appreciation “of his loving-kindness and goodness “together with the realization that he is the Supreme Judge and the Almighty.” So how does one come to have this awe of God?
Well, as the text says today that we’ve already read, ‘we first hear about his fame and about all he has done.’ Now, it’s of interest that just simply hearing and knowing what he does does not necessarily lead to awe or to the kind of awe that will help us develop a relationship with him.
The Scriptures explain this if you go to Joshua chapter 9, where we read about a group of Canaanite kings.
In Joshua chapter 9 and in verse 1, it says: “When all the kings who were on the west side of the Jordan heard what had happened.” Well, what did they hear that had happened?
Well, of course, the stories of the Exodus of Israel out of Egypt was one thing they heard and also the mighty acts of Jehovah God in connection with his people.
And then when Israel first penetrated Canaan after crossing the Jordan, they set up a camp at Gilgal, which was not far from the walled city of Jericho.
And these Canaanite kings had heard about what Jehovah did at Jericho —the great destruction there in behalf of his people.
And remember, Rahab was spared, and her family was spared, because when she heard about the mighty acts of Jehovah God, it moved her to put faith in him.
Now, if you just glance through Joshua chapter 8, we find that following that great destruction, they went to Ai —the city of Ai.
And Jehovah God had instructed them how to ambush that city, which led to the destruction there.
So after hearing those acts of Jehovah and then many other acts of Jehovah, what did it do to the Canaanite kings?
Were they moved with awe?
Well, let’s read chapter 9, verse 2.
It says: “They formed an alliance to fight against Joshua and Israel.” So it had the opposite effect upon them, didn’t it?
But now, notice the different reaction it had upon the Gibeonites.
Now, they heard the very same reports, and in chapter 9 and verse 9, it says: “At this they said to him: “‘Your servants have come from a very distant land “‘out of regard for the name of Jehovah your God, “‘because we have heard about his fame “‘and about all he did in Egypt and about all he did to the two kings of the Amorites.’” So in this case, they heard it and it led to their awe, which led to their developing a relationship with Jehovah God.
Well, today, the same thing is true that the mighty acts of Jehovah God are all around us.
And those that are “rightly disposed” recognize them and are moved to this awe that leads to a good relationship with Jehovah God.
We have a number of experiences in this regard that are related to the relief efforts going on right now.
I’d like to share one of them with you.
It was in the middle of the night, Monday, October 9, 2017, and the wildfires began to engulf areas around the city of Santa Rosa, California.
The elders began to call, text, and even personally alert those in the congregation that they needed to evacuate immediately.
And in time, hundreds of friends made their way to the Kingdom Hall, and from there arrangements were made for these displaced publishers to be housed with their brothers and sisters in unaffected areas, especially to the south.
Well, there was one Spanish family that hesitated to accept the hospitality that was being extended to them, and they did not want to be relocated.
And upon the loving inquiries of the elders, it was learned that the father was a non-Witness and he felt uncomfortable staying in another Witness home.
And he was embarrassed at the way he looked, the way he was dressed, and how dirty he was because of the circumstances.
So he requested to stay overnight at the Kingdom Hall with his family.
Well, temporary beds were set up at the Kingdom Hall.
And during the night, the circuit overseer had children singing Kingdom songs on the platform which really encouraged all.
The next morning, after experiencing the loving atmosphere in the Kingdom Hall and seeing how well Jehovah took care of this man’s family, as well as the other publishers, the man stated that he was going to call the brother back who studied with him many years ago and he would resume his Bible study and he would never ever miss a meeting again.
Yes, those “rightly disposed” become in awe of Jehovah God, and that moves them to have a good relationship with him.
Remember, ‘the whole obligation of man is to fear God.’ So the question is, Now that we have developed this relationship with Jehovah God, how do we keep it?
Well, for sure, we must continue to be observant of Jehovah’s mighty acts in connection not only with ourselves but in connection with his people worldwide.
Never should we allow the wonderful experiences that we hear —the outstanding World Service Report or the support and comfort that Jehovah God is giving those in need— to become commonplace to us.
We must stay in awe of our God and Father.
Now, you remember the first scripture we read in Ecclesiastes where it says that in addition to fearing the true God, we’re told to keep his commandments.
So it would be good to ask ourselves this question: ‘What commandment of Jehovah God do I have difficulty keeping?
‘Is it a commandment that involves my self-control, ‘my zeal, ‘my humility, or perhaps my honesty?’ If so, then we should give immediate attention to improving the spiritual person that we are so that keeping God’s commandments will become as easy as being in awe of what Jehovah is doing in behalf of his people.
Brothers and sisters, we stand at the threshold of the promised new world, and may we heed the words here at Hebrews 12:28 (by not only developing but by keeping a relationship with Jehovah God), where it says —Hebrews 12:28: “Therefore, seeing that we are to receive “a Kingdom that cannot be shaken, “let us continue to receive undeserved kindness, “through which we may acceptably offer God sacred service with godly fear and awe.”
